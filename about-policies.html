Ltd. as a free service exclusively for those users of the gcom.net.au, castlemaine.net, centralvictoria.net and vicmail.net email domains with existing and financially current email accounts as at 15th June, 2026.

Email and Spam

Clients are required to regularly collect and/or remove all email stored in all mailboxes and folders associated with their accounts. The gcom.net.au service will be the sole arbiter in determining violations of this provision.

Mailbox disk space limits of 200MB apply to all email accounts.

Email items older than two months will be regularly purged from all folders of all email accounts.

Email items in Trash, Deleted, Spam and Junk folders will be treated as immediately available for purging.

The gcom.net.au service takes a zero tolerance approach to the sending of Unsolicited Commercial Email (UCE) or spam over its network, or over any other network by way of any service provided by or associated with gcom.net.au. Very simply, this means that Clients of gcom.net.au may not use or permit others to use its network to transact in UCE, and may not use or permit others to use any external network to transact in UCE by way of any service provided by or associated with gcom.net.au. Violations of this policy carry severe penalties, including termination of service.

Upon detection or notification of an alleged violation of the spam policy, gcom.net.au will initiate an immediate investigation. During the investigation, gcom.net.au may restrict the Client's access to the network to prevent further violations. If the Client is found to be in violation of the spam policy, gcom.net.au may, at its sole discretion, restrict, suspend or terminate the Client's account. Further, gcom.net.au reserves the right to pursue civil remedies for any costs associated with the investigation of a substantiated policy violation. The gcom.net.au service will notify law enforcement officials if the violation is believed to be a criminal offence.

Clients who violate this policy agree that they will pay research fees not to exceed AU$220 per hour for each hour or part thereof which gcom.net.au personnel must spend in investigating the matter. Severe violations of this policy will result in the immediate termination of the Client's account and may incur an administrative fee of up to AU$1500.

We consider UCE or spam to be any email message for which a spam complaint is raised by a message recipient, or a valid SMTP rejection or bounce message is recieved, and where the Client cannot produce proof of double opting in on the part of that recipient.

Quite simply, to avoid any problems or costs, do not spam from, through, or in connection with your gcom.net.au account.

The Client may not use any service provided by gcom.net.au as a point of email origin for anything other than low-level email despatch which is not associated in any way with mailing lists.

A maximum daily email send limit of the greater of 300 email items or 300 email recipients applies to all email accounts.
